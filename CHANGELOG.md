### Added
- UK Sportive Difficulty Calculator at `/calculators/health/uk-sportive-difficulty-calculator/`: cycling sibling of the marathon course difficulty calculator. Drop in a GPX file, set rider + bike weight and either a target flat-road speed or sustained power output, get a physics-based finish time, average speed, the categorised climbs flagged with predicted arrival times, top descent speed, and steepest gradient. Uses the standard cycling-power equation `P × η = (m·g·sinθ + m·g·cosθ·Crr)·v + 0.5·ρ·CdA·v³` solved per segment by bisection. Rider-type dropdown picks sensible CdA (drag area) and Crr (rolling resistance) defaults from recreational (CdA 0.40), through club (0.32), to racer (0.27). Descents steeper than -1.5% modelled as coasting (terminal velocity from gravity vs aero/rolling drag), capped at 80 km/h as a real-world safety ceiling. Strava's *original* cycling categorisation thresholds (length × grade ≥ 8000 = Cat 4, scaling up to HC at ≥ 80000), not the lowered floor we used for runners. No late-race fatigue weighting because cyclists refuel on the bike. Power input wins if both speed and power are supplied. UK localisation stack (slug prefix, hreflang en-GB + x-default, Schema.org `inLanguage: en-GB` + `countriesSupported: GB`). Pure-logic `uk-sportive-difficulty.js` module with 27 unit tests covering haversine, smoothing, GPX parsing, the power-to-speed solver across grade ranges, the inverse flat-speed-to-power, coasting terminal velocity, climb detection and Strava-cycling categorisation, difficulty rating, and end-to-end calculations on synthetic flat and hilly courses. Cross-linked from the UK Marathon Course Difficulty Calculator
- UK Marathon Course Difficulty Calculator at `/calculators/health/uk-marathon-course-difficulty-calculator/`: drop in a GPX file for any UK marathon and a target flat pace, get a terrain-adjusted finish time, the toughest sections of the course flagged with predicted arrival time, total ascent / descent, and a difficulty rating (flat / rolling / hilly / brutal). Strava-style empirical Grade-Adjusted Pace curve (caps downhill benefit at -9%, no benefit past -18%, penalty thereafter) plus position-in-race fatigue weighting that penalises late-race uphills and applies an eccentric-damage penalty to steep late-race downhills (the "Comrades Down Run" effect). Marathon-experience selector scales how hard the late-race fatigue hits. GPX parsed entirely in-browser via FileReader and a regex tokeniser, no API calls, no upload. Strava-style climb categorisation with the floor lowered from 8000 to 4000 because runners care about smaller hills than cyclists. UK localisation stack (slug prefix, hreflang en-GB + x-default, Schema.org `inLanguage: en-GB` + `countriesSupported: GB`). Pure-logic `uk-marathon-course-difficulty.js` module with 30 unit tests covering haversine, smoothing, GAP curve shape, fatigue weighting, GPX parsing, climb detection, categorisation, difficulty rating, and end-to-end calculations on synthetic flat and hilly courses. Cross-linked from the Running Pace Calculator
